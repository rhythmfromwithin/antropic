### PM Culture & How Products Get Built at Anthropic

Anthropic's product development approach diverges sharply from conventional PM practice:

1. **Product Notes, not PRDs.** Three paragraphs: problem + evidence, hypothesis, success signal. No 10-page requirement docs.
2. **45-minute prototype cycle.** PM writes Product Note → uses Claude Code to produce a working PR → ships to internal dogfood → measures. Total cycle: 1–3 days.
3. **Side quests are canonical.** Claude Code on Desktop, AskUserQuestion, and todo lists all started as informal experiments outside the roadmap. PMs have explicit license to explore.
4. **Dogfood is the first deployment.** All of Anthropic (~800+ staff) uses new features before external launch. Internal engagement is the primary early signal.
5. **Do the simple thing.** Principle: any feature that works around a model limitation becomes technical debt at the next model drop. Design for the model you'll have in 6 months.
6. **No 12-month roadmaps.** Rolling 6-week cycles. Exponentially improving models break annual planning assumptions.
7. **Demo culture.** Working artifacts replace status updates. Alignment happens through things people can use, not slides people can debate.

## Section 2: Problem Framing Canvas (MITRE v3)

*Framework: Look Inward → Look Outward → Reframe. Produces a bias-resistant problem statement and "How Might We" question before solutions are considered.*

---

### Phase 1: Look Inward

**What is the problem? (Symptoms)**

The Claude Platform API (platform.claude.com) is growing at 70× YoY in volume but developer community breadth significantly trails the market leader (OpenAI: ~4M developers). This suggests high intensity per developer but limited top-of-funnel reach. The self-serve funnel — from signup to first API call to paid conversion — has not been the primary PM focus while Anthropic was in its research-first, enterprise-first phase. The PM, API Growth role is being created now precisely because this gap is becoming strategic.

**Why hasn't this been solved yet?**

1. **It was deliberately deprioritized.** Anthropic spent 2021–2024 on model development and enterprise sales. Self-serve PLG funnel optimization was not survival-critical when enterprise revenue was growing rapidly.
2. **It's genuinely hard.** Competing with OpenAI's 4M-developer head start and Google's price-per-token advantage requires a differentiated PLG motion, not just feature parity.
3. **The role didn't exist.** API Growth as a dedicated PM function is new — which means no institutional playbook yet exists internally.
4. **It may be misframed.** "How do we get more developers?" might be the wrong question. The deeper question may be "How do we get developers who build things that go to production?" — a quality-over-volume framing.

**How are we part of the problem? (Assumptions to challenge)**

- **Assumption 1:** We assume the bottleneck is acquisition (not enough new signups). It may actually be activation (signups who never make a second API call) or expansion (developers who plateau at low usage).
- **Assumption 2:** We assume developers compare Claude to OpenAI on price. Many developers compare Claude to Llama 4/5 on *cost-to-zero* (self-hosted open weights). Our funnel may be losing before the developer even reaches the signup page.
- **Assumption 3:** We optimize for English-speaking developers in the US/EU. International developer communities (India, Brazil, Southeast Asia) are large and growing — and underserved by our current onboarding.
- **Assumption 4:** We assume a developer who signs up is ready to build. Many are evaluating — they want to *test* Claude quality vs. GPT-5 before committing. Our console may not be optimized for the evaluator journey, only the builder journey.

---

### Phase 2: Look Outward

**Who experiences the problem? When, where, and with what consequences?**

| Segment | When they hit friction | Where | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Indie/hobbyist developer** | At signup — API key setup, billing setup, rate limits | Console onboarding | Abandons; tries GPT-4o instead; never returns |
| **Startup CTO (pre-Series A)** | At first production use — reliability SLAs, pricing predictability | API docs + billing | Chooses OpenAI or Bedrock for "safer" default |
| **Enterprise architect** | At procurement — missing audit logs, data residency, SSO | Sales-to-self-serve gap | Requires sales involvement for what should be self-serve |
| **Developer evaluating models** | During trial — no easy A/B comparison tooling vs. other models | Console / playground | Defaults to whichever model is easier to test |
| **International developer** | At onboarding — English-only content, USD billing, US-centric examples | Docs + pricing page | Chooses a local or more accessible alternative |

**Who else has this problem?**

- **Stripe (2011–2015):** Faced the same "developers understand, enterprises don't trust" dynamic. Solved it with radically simplified integration (7 lines of code) and a pricing page that was honest about costs. Result: developers became Stripe's enterprise sales force.
- **Twilio (2010–2016):** API adoption at low developer penetration vs. legacy telcos. Solved with free tier, exhaustive cookbooks, and a "send your first SMS in 5 minutes" activation hook.
- **Vercel:** Competes with AWS/GCP. Wins on time-to-deployed-URL — activation is the moat.

Common pattern: the fastest-growing developer platforms solved *activation time*, not marketing spend.

**Who doesn't have this problem?**

- **Developers already deeply embedded in the OpenAI ecosystem** — they've built switching costs through SDK integrations, prompting patterns, and organizational knowledge.
- **AWS Bedrock customers** — they don't choose a model, they consume one through their existing infrastructure. The "problem" is abstracted away by AWS.
- **Meta Llama users** — they have a different problem (infra complexity) but not a signup/activation funnel problem. The model is free; the friction is operational, not commercial.

**Who's been left out of the conversation?**

- **Non-technical founders** who want to build AI products but don't identify as "developers" — Claude's reasoning capability is uniquely accessible to them, but the API console is optimized for engineers.
- **Academic researchers** who have budget constraints and need free/low-cost tiers with generous usage limits.
- **Developers in emerging markets** for whom USD pricing and US-centric payment infrastructure is a real barrier.
- **Junior developers** who are learning to build — the first experience with an AI API shapes long-term platform loyalty; Anthropic is not present in most developer education journeys.

**Who benefits when the activation problem exists?**

- OpenAI: every developer who fails to activate on Claude stays in the GPT ecosystem.
- Internal teams: don't have to build and maintain self-serve onboarding infrastructure.
- Sales team: large enterprise deals bypass self-serve entirely, so a broken self-serve funnel doesn't hurt their quota.

**Who loses if the problem is solved?**

- The enterprise sales motion partially — if self-serve self-serves itself through $100K ARR, some deals never reach the sales team at all.
- *(This is a feature, not a bug: NRR is better when expansion is self-serve.)*

---

### Phase 3: Reframe

**Stated another way, the problem is:**

> Developers who would prefer Claude's reasoning quality and safety properties cannot activate quickly enough to form a usage habit before the evaluation window closes — because the current onboarding flow assumes a "builder" (someone who has already decided to build) rather than an "evaluator" (someone who is comparing Claude to alternatives). This disproportionately affects developers at the evaluation stage, international developers, and non-technical builders — and has been overlooked because Anthropic's growth has come primarily from enterprise contracts and Claude Code, not self-serve API adoption.

**How Might We statement:**

> **How might we** help any developer — regardless of technical level, geography, or prior AI experience — reach their first working Claude integration in under 3 minutes, so they can experience the quality and safety difference before choosing a competitor?

*This HMW statement sets up the right solution space: it's about activation speed and universality, not just marketing volume.*

## Section 6: Opportunity Solution Tree (Full)

*Framework: Teresa Torres, Continuous Discovery Habits. Outcome → Opportunities → Solutions → Assumption Tests. The tree ends at tests, not solutions.*

---

### Desired Outcome

**Increase API Platform Activation Rate**
*(% of new signups who make their first successful API call within 24 hours of account creation)*

**Why this metric:**
- Activation is the single most predictive metric for D30 retention in developer PLG products
- It's directly influenceable by the API Growth PM without requiring model improvements or enterprise sales changes
- It sets a lagging metric (paid conversion) in motion — you cannot monetize a developer who hasn't activated

**Target:** Move from current baseline (unknown — measure in Week 1) to top-quartile PLG benchmark (>50% of signups activating within 24h)

**Why it matters:** At 70× YoY API volume growth, a 10pp improvement in activation rate compounds dramatically. If 10,000 developers sign up monthly and activation improves from 30% to 40%, that's 1,000 additional activated developers per month — each of whom is now on a compounding usage and expansion curve.

---

### Opportunity Map

---

#### Opportunity A: Developer gets stuck before making the first API call

**Problem:** New signups know they want to use Claude but don't have a clear first step. The console routes them to documentation; documentation routes them back to the console; neither surface provides a zero-friction "run this and it works" entry point.

**Evidence:**
- Competitor benchmark: OpenAI Playground allows live API execution at signup, within the browser, with no setup
- Industry standard: Stripe's "7 lines of code" and Twilio's "5-minute SMS" benchmarks exist because they proved time-to-value is the primary activation driver
- Supporting: Average developer attention window for evaluating a new API is estimated at 15 minutes or less before abandoning

**Solutions:**

| Solution | Description | Assumption Test |
|---|---|---|
| **A1: In-console interactive quickstart** | Pre-loaded working API call in console onboarding; "Run this" button; live response displayed | "If developers can execute a working API call in the console without leaving, TTFAC <5 min rate will increase ≥10pp" |
| **A2: Use-case-first onboarding** | Onboarding asks "what are you building?" and routes to a pre-configured starting template (chatbot, document analysis, code review, etc.) | "If we match developers to a starting template based on stated use case, D7 retention will increase vs. blank-slate onboarding" |
| **A3: Evaluator-first console mode** | Separate entry path for "I'm comparing Claude to GPT/Gemini" vs. "I'm building something new" — serves comparison prompts and side-by-side output displays | "Developers in evaluation mode have different onboarding needs than builders; separating these paths will reduce early drop-off" |

**Recommended POC: A1 (In-console interactive quickstart)**
- Feasibility: High (UI change only, no model work)
- Impact: High (TTFAC is the primary activation lever)
- Market Fit: High (every major competitor offers this; developers expect it)
- Score: 4/5, 4/5, 5/5 = 13 total
- Build: 2 weeks → internal dogfood → A/B at 20% of new signups

---

#### Opportunity B: Developer is activated but doesn't have a reason to upgrade

**Problem:** Developers who successfully make their first API call often plateau at free-tier usage. The moment of highest upgrade motivation — when a hard limit is hit — is currently met with an error message, not an upgrade offer. The billing page exists but requires proactive navigation.

**Evidence:**
- Free → paid conversion for developer API products: industry benchmark 3–5%
- The contextual upgrade pattern (upgrade CTA at moment of friction) is documented in PLG literature as increasing conversion 20–40% vs. passive billing page approach
- Rate limits, token limits, and feature gates are natural upgrade trigger moments that currently go unutilized

**Solutions:**

| Solution | Description | Assumption Test |
|---|---|---|
| **B1: Contextual upgrade modal at limit surfaces** | When a free developer hits a rate limit, token limit, or feature gate, display "You've hit the limit — here's what upgrading unlocks" with a one-click upgrade path | "Surfacing upgrade CTA at peak motivation (limit-hit moment) will increase free→paid conversion by ≥20% vs. billing-page-only approach" |
| **B2: Usage dashboard with upgrade suggestion** | Proactive "you're at 80% of your monthly limit" notification with "here's what the next tier gets you" comparison | "Proactive usage notifications reduce surprise limit-hits and increase planned upgrades" |
| **B3: Trial of paid features** | 48-hour trial of premium capabilities (higher rate limits, larger context, priority queue) triggered after first successful API call | "Developers who experience premium capabilities during trial upgrade at higher rates than those who don't" |

**Recommended POC: B1 (Contextual upgrade modal)**
- Feasibility: High (UI + billing API integration; no backend infrastructure)
- Impact: High (captures intent at peak motivation)
- Market Fit: High (well-documented PLG pattern across Notion, Linear, Vercel, etc.)
- Score: 4/5, 5/5, 5/5 = 14 total
- Build: 2 weeks → A/B test → measure paid conversion delta

| **Engineering capacity consumed by model infrastructure** | High | High | Ship UI-only wins first (no backend) to build PM credibility before requesting eng allocation |
| **Meta Llama 5 (free, frontier-class) pulls cost-sensitive developers** | High | High | Target regulated verticals and production-reliability use cases where free open-source is not a viable option |
| **Google's $2/M token pricing creates permanent price pressure** | Medium | High | Compete on safety posture, model quality ceiling, and MCP/Conway ecosystem — not on price |
| **Revenue accounting dispute (gross vs. net) creates metric noise** | Low | Medium | Keep PM-owned metrics (TTFAC, activation rate, paid conversion) clean and independent of revenue recognition choices |
| **Claude Code → API assumption invalidated (>80% already converted)** | Medium | Medium | Validate assumption in Week 1 before building C1; redirect effort to expansion if true |
| **Anthropic-Bedrock channel conflict intensifies** | Medium | Medium | Map which enterprise segments prefer Bedrock vs. direct; don't fight the channel for AWS-native buyers |
| **Enterprise self-serve expansion is blocked by procurement, not product** | Medium | High | Interview Thesis B accounts in Week 2; if procurement is the block, Thesis B is wrong and shift to A or C |
