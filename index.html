Platform Thesis Candidates Section 8

Select one by Day 30. Experiments validate the thesis — they don't originate it.

Thesis A

MCP Ecosystem as Distribution Moat

Anthropic invented MCP. Position platform.claude.com as the canonical place to discover, publish, and monetize MCP servers. Developers who publish extensions develop increasing switching costs.

Key assumption: Developers are building MCP servers without a canonical marketplace. Validate: informal directories, GitHub repos, community lists that pre-date official Marketplace.
Thesis B

Enterprise Self-Serve Expansion ($10K → $1M ARR)

$1M+ ARR accounts scaled 12 → 500+. Biggest unlock is expansion — removing the sales rep at the $50K–$200K tier via usage dashboards, team management, SSO, RBAC, and audit logs.

Key assumption: $10K–$100K accounts stall due to missing tooling, not pricing or performance. Validate: interview 10 accounts in this tier about expansion blockers.
Thesis C

Safety as a Paid Growth Feature for Regulated Verticals

Finance, healthcare, and government are underserved by OpenAI and Google. Constitutional AI is real differentiation — but platform tooling (audit logs, data residency, RBAC) doesn't yet match the safety story.

Key assumption: Regulated-industry developers are blocked by missing platform controls, not procurement complexity. Validate: do they cite product gaps or process gaps as primary barrier?
